# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Scottish founders, fundraising directors, and ecosystem observers — people who want sharp intelligence on who is actively deploying capital in Scotland. Read on Monday mornings before meetings. Sophisticated and time-pressed; they want signal, not polish.

## Product Purpose

A weekly intelligence briefing and searchable data platform monitoring public news sources for VC investment activity in Scottish startups and scaleups. Success means a reader can walk into a fundraising conversation knowing which firms are writing cheques, at what stages, and with what cadence — without having to stitch together the picture themselves.

## Brand Personality

Precise, local, unfiltered. Not a VC marketing channel. A sharp analyst's briefing that happens to be free.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard** — navy + warm-near-white + muted gold. Could be any tool. This is the current problem.
- **VC marketing site** — gradient heroes, hero-metric templates, promotional tone.
- **Scottish cliché** — tartan, heather purple, thistle iconography. Identity should not scream Scotland.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over decoration.** Every design choice should make the data clearer or the authority stronger — not add visual interest for its own sake.
2. **Specificity builds trust.** Exact numbers, named sources, attributed quotes. The design should mirror this: no vague treatments, no approximate colours.
3. **Local without being parochial.** Feel distinctively Scottish without deploying the visual symbols. The specificity of the content IS the local identity.
4. **Readable at pace.** Users are time-pressed. Hierarchy should let a reader extract the key facts without reading every word.
5. **No corporate gloss.** The unfiltered personality requires visual restraint: no gradients, no hero animations, no promotional treatments.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum throughout. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast. Data tables must be legible without relying on colour alone (stage and sector differentiators need shape or label alongside colour).
