# Scottish VC Tracker — 22 June 2026

## This Week

- A £1.2m round for JET Connectivity, led by Tricapital, surfaced this week via Angel Capital Scotland — but the deal itself dates back to early May. The company builds infrastructure-independent 5G connectivity for hard-to-reach offshore and coastal sites.
- Scotland's biotech scene gained a new name: Bead BioPharma emerged from stealth mode this week, backed by Archangels, after filing its first patent for an antibody-drug conjugate cancer platform. No funding amount was disclosed alongside the announcement.
- Otherwise a quiet week for fresh deal flow — most of the activity behind this issue is confirmation and tidying of rounds already on the board (Wordsmith AI's Series B, Aveni's growth round, the Tricapital trio of HonuWorx, Kaly and Sisaltech, and others), rather than new news.

## The Numbers

Q2 (April–June) stands at 19 deals and £107.5m, up from 18 deals and £106.5m last issue — a delta of one deal and roughly £1.2m, entirely down to the JET Connectivity round above. Year to date, Scotland has now seen 29 deals worth £141.6m, up from 28 deals and £140.5m.

**Most active investors this quarter** — by frequency: Tricapital (4 deals), Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund (3), then a tie between Maven Capital Partners and STAC Invest (2 deals each). By capital: Highland Europe (£51.9m), Index Ventures (£51.9m), Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund (£22.5m).

Seed and pre-seed rounds still make up the bulk of Q2 deal count, but two outsized raises carry the capital — Wordsmith AI's Series B and Aveni's growth round between them account for well over half of the quarter's total. AI and deep tech are the sectors showing up most often across the quarter, with life sciences close behind. Edinburgh continues to draw the lion's share of activity, Glasgow is a clear second, and the rest is scattered across the Borders and other parts of Scotland.

## Deal Spotlight

### JET Connectivity — undisclosed round type — £1.2m

**Lead investor**: Tricapital · **Co-investors**: The FSE Group, plus unnamed private investors · **Sector**: Deep Tech · **Location**: unconfirmed

JET Connectivity has built a land- and sea-based alternative to satellite networks like Starlink, aimed at bringing secure, high-bandwidth connectivity to offshore, coastal and ultra-remote sites that conventional infrastructure can't reach. Tricapital led a £1.2m round into the company, with The FSE Group and private investors also participating, money the company says will let it scale through 2026 to meet demand from national security and resilience-focused customers. The deal first appeared in Angel Capital Scotland coverage back in early May, but only reached this run's data; the round type itself is described inconsistently across the source material, and it's still being treated as a single deal pending a closer look at the conflicting reports. Scotland's connection to the company isn't fully pinned down either — it's described as UK-developed without a confirmed headquarters location.

Source: Angel Capital Scotland (angelcapital.scot)
