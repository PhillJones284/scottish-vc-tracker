# Scottish VC Tracker — 8 June 2026

## This Week

- Highland Europe and Index Ventures led a $70m (£51.87m) Series B into Wordsmith AI, the Edinburgh legal AI startup — one of the largest Scottish tech deals in recent memory.
- PXN Ventures led a £12m growth round into Aveni, the Edinburgh fintech building AI conduct-risk monitoring for financial services, with Lloyds Banking Group and Nationwide both returning as backers.
- Mercia Ventures led a $5m (£3.7m) seed round into Go Swag, a Glasgow corporate-gifting platform, alongside Techstart Ventures.
- Outward VC led an undisclosed pre-seed round into Infrawatch, an Edinburgh cybersecurity startup building infrastructure intelligence tools — announced in early May but only surfacing in our sources this week.
- Bead BioPharma, an Archangels-backed antibody-drug conjugate developer, came out of stealth this week after filing its first patent; no new funding round was announced alongside it.

## The Numbers

This is the first issue — here's where things stand. Year to date, 28 deals have closed across Scotland totalling £140.5m. Q2 (April–June) accounts for 18 of those deals and £106.5m, against 10 deals and £34.1m in Q1.

**Most active investors this quarter** — by frequency: Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund (6 deals), Tricapital (3), then a tie between Maven Capital Partners and STAC Invest (2 deals each). By capital: Highland Europe (£51.9m), Index Ventures (£51.9m), Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund (£24.3m).

Seed and pre-seed rounds dominate by volume — eleven of this quarter's eighteen deals — but growth and Series B rounds carry the weight on capital, with Wordsmith AI's Series B alone accounting for nearly half of Q2's total. Deep tech and life sciences are the most repeated sectors this quarter, while Edinburgh continues to draw the bulk of activity (eight of eighteen Q2 deals), with Glasgow and the wider Borders/Highlands region splitting the rest fairly evenly.

## Deal Spotlight

### Wordsmith AI — Series B — £51.87m
**Lead investor**: Highland Europe · **Co-investors**: Index Ventures · **Sector**: AI & Machine Learning, SaaS & Enterprise Software · **Location**: Edinburgh

Wordsmith AI, the Edinburgh legal AI startup founded in 2023 by Ross McNairn, Volodymyr Giginiak and Robbie Falkenthal, raised $70m, taking total funding to $100m. The company has grown to 130 staff serving more than 500 enterprise customers, including BT, the Financial Times and Canva. The round was led by Highland Europe — a London growth investor whose portfolio includes n8n, Camunda and Wolt — alongside Index Ventures. The capital will fund expansion to around 300 staff globally and a deeper push into the US market.

Source: Daily Business Group (https://dailybusinessgroup.co.uk/2026/06/wordsmith-ai-raises-52-million-amid-rapid-growth/)

### Aveni — Growth — £12m
**Lead investor**: PXN Ventures · **Co-investors**: Puma Growth Partners, Lloyds Banking Group, Nationwide, Scottish Enterprise Investment Fund · **Sector**: AI & Machine Learning, Fintech · **Location**: Edinburgh

Aveni, a University of Edinburgh spinout, raised £12m to develop its Unified Assurance Platform, which monitors AI conduct risk for financial services firms. The round is notable for its backers: both Lloyds Banking Group and Nationwide returned as investors, alongside long-time backer Puma Growth Partners, suggesting the UK's biggest retail banks see Aveni's compliance tooling as more than a one-off bet. Aveni also featured on Sifted's 2026 list of the UK and Ireland's fastest-growing businesses.

Source: Scottish Financial Review (https://scottishfinancialreview.com/2026/06/04/aveni-edinburgh-ai-wealth-startup-raises-12m/)
