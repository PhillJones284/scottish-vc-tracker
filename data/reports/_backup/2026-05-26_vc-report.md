# Scottish VC Investment Intelligence Report
**Week ending 26 May 2026**

## 1. Executive Summary

**15 confirmed investment records** across 15 Scottish companies, representing an estimated **£64.2m in capital deployed** (including USD-denominated rounds converted at current rates). One additional low-confidence record (ePass) is excluded from totals and discussed in the appendix.

**Headline observations:**

- **Edinburgh dominates at the large end.** Three Edinburgh-based spinouts — Exergy3 (£10m), Bioliberty (£10.5m Series A), and Earth Blox (£6m) — account for 41% of total capital in this period. All three are university spinouts, reinforcing Edinburgh's position as Scotland's deep-tech commercialisation engine.
- **Glasgow is more active by deal count.** Six of the 15 high/medium-confidence deals were Glasgow companies, but at smaller average cheque sizes. STAC Invest backed three Glasgow companies in a single cohort announcement, while EnteroBiotix's £19m growth round is a significant outlier for the city.
- **The Investment Fund for Scotland (managed by Maven) appears in four deals**, making it the most consistently active capital source this period. Maven and the IFS have invested in companies ranging from a road QA startup to a live entertainment platform — the breadth is striking.
- **Two significant caveats:** The date range covered is broad (January–May 2026) rather than a single week. Deal density peaks in March–April 2026. Some records carry `location_unknown` or `company_not_clearly_scottish` flags — see the appendix.

## 6. Sector Heat Map

**Deep Tech** is the dominant category by deal count (5 records: Cnuic, Quantcore, Airspection, Bioliberty, Vuabl), though several other deals have deeptech characteristics — photonic semiconductors, biochar, robotic surgery. Glasgow and Edinburgh are both active here, with a pattern of university spinouts at pre-seed and seed stages.

**Energy & Cleantech** is the largest by capital in this period: Exergy3 (£10m) and Carbogenics (~£2.22m) together with the climate-adjacent Earth Blox deal suggest strong momentum. Industrial decarbonisation is the emerging sub-theme — Exergy3's thermal storage and Carbogenics' biochar both target industrial carbon reduction rather than consumer cleantech.

**Life Sciences / Healthtech** is the most consistent performer across the date range: Biocaptiva (seed), Bioliberty (Series A), EnteroBiotix (growth), Nami Surgical (growth), and Entourage AI (pre-seed) span the full funding lifecycle. Edinburgh spinouts dominate the earlier stages; Glasgow is making a push at growth stage via EnteroBiotix and Nami.

**Climate Tech / Nature Risk** is emerging as a distinct sub-sector via Earth Blox, which sits at the intersection of climate data and financial services. ESG data platforms serving financial institutions are attracting institutional capital (PXN, European Space Agency) — this is a watch sector.

**Property & Construction Tech** appears via Vuabl and Swurf — both small pre-seed deals targeting adjacent problems (housing inspection, flexible workspace). Neither is large enough to confirm a trend, but the social housing angle of Vuabl could attract public funding alongside private capital.

**Sectors absent from this period:** No fintech deals, no SaaS/enterprise software as a primary sector, no agritech, no space tech as a standalone. This may reflect source coverage gaps rather than absence of activity.
