# Scottish VC Intelligence Report — 8 June 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This report covers **13 named investment records** at high or medium confidence, drawn from a deduped dataset of 20 records (166 raw inputs). Four additional medium-confidence records carry no company name or investor data and are relegated to the appendix. Three further records are low-confidence and similarly excluded from primary analysis.

**Estimated capital deployed (high/medium confidence, named companies): approximately £95m**, dominated by a single large deal. Excluding Wordsmith AI's £51.87m Series B, the remaining 12 named deals total approximately £43m across a spread of seed to growth rounds.

**Headline observations:**

1. **Wordsmith AI's £51.87m Series B is the standout deal of the period** — one of the largest single Scottish tech raises on record. Edinburgh-headquartered, three years old, 130 staff, 500+ enterprise customers. Highland Europe and Index Ventures led. This is a marquee deal for the Scottish ecosystem and signals that London-tier growth capital is now backing Edinburgh AI companies at scale.

2. **PXN Ventures is the most active named institutional VC this period with four deals** — leading Earth Blox (£6m Series A), Aveni (£12m growth), co-leading Quantcore (£2.5m seed), and joining Launchpad's $11m Series A. The Edinburgh-based firm, formed from the 2025 merger of Par Equity and Praetura Ventures, appears to be deploying at pace across deep tech, AI, and cleantech.

3. **Life sciences and healthtech dominate early-stage activity.** Five of the thirteen named deals involve companies in Healthtech or Life Sciences: Lentitek, Bioliberty, Biocaptiva, Gigged.AI (adjacent — AI for technical hiring), and the low-confidence Bead BioPharma. Edinburgh is the hub. The pattern of university spinouts (Edinburgh, Glasgow) raising angel and institutional rounds at seed is consistent and strengthening.

**Caveats:** The date range covered is wide — records span from approximately February to early June 2026, with several undated. Four medium-confidence records with data quality scores of 10/100 contain no actionable investment data and are noted in the appendix. The Gigged.AI record uses the legacy name "Par Equity" rather than PXN Ventures — this may represent a pre-merger deal or a data artefact.

**Pre-Seed:** Only one named deal (Infrawatch), with amount undisclosed. The amount was described as "significant backing" and described as "millions" in the source, but no figure was confirmed. Outward VC led, alongside Triple Point Ventures and Portfolio Ventures. Activity at this stage is likely higher than the data reflects — pre-seed deals are consistently underreported in press coverage.

**Seed:** The busiest stage by deal count, with seven transactions ranging from £200k (Swurf) to £3.7m (Go Swag). PXN Ventures co-led Quantcore; Archangels led Biocaptiva; Equity Gap led Lentitek; Maven led Highway Data Systems; Mercia Ventures led Go Swag. The stage is characterised by syndication — multiple angels and public-sector co-investors (Scottish Enterprise) feature in almost every deal.

**Series A:** Three deals totalling approximately £20.4m. PXN led Earth Blox (£6m); SNIB led Bioliberty (£6.2m); Launchpad's $11m (£8.15m) had no named lead in the data, though PXN and SNIB were confirmed participants. Series A is where the most institutional capital concentration appears, and where Edinburgh companies are finding multiple routes to funding.

**Series B:** A single deal, but the largest of the period — Wordsmith AI's £51.87m raise led by Highland Europe and Index Ventures. This is significant: neither firm is a regular presence in the Scottish ecosystem at this scale. Their entry may signal that Edinburgh's AI sector is now on the radar of London's Tier 1 growth investors.

**Growth:** Aveni's £12m round led by PXN Ventures, with Puma Growth Partners, Lloyds, and Nationwide as co-investors. The presence of two UK high-street banks as investors in an AI assurance platform is an unusual but telling signal — strategic investment from potential customers rather than pure financial capital.
